<DESCRIPTION>
I introduce four targeted improvements to reduce makespan by focusing on real conflict costs rather than proxy heuristics:

1) Shared prefix-dominance and child-level greedy promotion in beam search:
   - Replace beam’s local dominance with the shared prefix_dom used by greedy, keyed by (frozenset(remaining), suffix). Use adaptive suffix length (3 until ~70% depth, 4 thereafter).
   - For each expanded parent, greedily complete the top K children by lookahead (K=2 early, K=1 later), prune children whose greedy completions are not better than the incumbent, and update the incumbent when improved. This tightens bounds earlier and drives stronger pruning.

2) Anti-buddy gating using a 75th percentile threshold:
   - Compute qcut thresholds per row of W.
   - In both beam and greedy construction, skip followers t after last if W[last][t] is large (above the 75th percentile) unless it’s very competitive. This avoids known-bad adjacencies and concentrates on high-yield continuations.

3) Anchored “bridge” move in LNS:
   - Target the worst violated adjacency (a,b), extract a short block anchored at b (size 3–5), and reinsert it just before a using a ΔW surrogate to pre-rank positions. This repairs boundary conflicts efficiently without extensive permutation.

4) Keep prior boundary-focused permutations, relocations, and adjacent hill-climbing intact.

These changes sharpen pruning, steer expansions away from conflict-heavy successors, and add a low-cost, conflict-targeted neighborhood, improving solution quality without excessive runtime.
