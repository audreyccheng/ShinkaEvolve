I introduce three synergistic improvements to the get_best_schedule function that are directly targeted at reducing makespan by focusing simulator calls on truly promising schedules and pruning weak branches early:

1) Deterministic multi-restart beam with shared memoized cache and incumbent-based pruning:
   - Run 2–3 beam configurations per workload, reusing a single cost_cache to avoid duplicate simulator calls.
   - Maintain a global incumbent full-schedule cost, updated via periodic greedy completions of top partial beam states, and prune child expansions whose immediate cost is already ≥ incumbent (monotonicity).
   - This tightens the search early and increases robustness to beam parameter choices.

2) Buddy lists to guide lookahead cheaply:
   - Precompute per-transaction buddy lists B[t] from pairwise costs M and preference margins W, capturing strong synergy/low-conflict partners.
   - During lookahead, prioritize next candidates from B[t] ∩ remaining before falling back to tournament-based preselection, leading to more accurate two-step evaluations with fewer calls.

3) Violation-focused local improvements:
   - In VNS, bias swap and relocate attempts toward boundaries where tournament preference prefer_before fails with large |W|; this concentrates effort on the most conflict-heavy adjacencies.
   - Keep overall budgets modest to ensure runtime remains reasonable.

These changes are consistent and build on the current code’s structure. They improve solution quality by tightening the search with a strong incumbent and by using W/M structure to better guide both construction and local refinement, which should lower the makespan on average.
