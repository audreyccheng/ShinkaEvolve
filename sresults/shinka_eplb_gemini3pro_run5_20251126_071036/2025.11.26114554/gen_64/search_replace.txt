<DESCRIPTION>
I am modifying the `balanced_packing` function to include an advanced "2-for-2" swap refinement alongside "1-for-1" swaps, integrated into the parallel randomized restart framework.

Rationale:
1.  **Limitation of 1-Swap**: Simple 1-item swaps often get stuck in local optima where no single item exchange improves balance, but swapping a pair of items from the heaviest pack with a pair from the lightest pack would.
2.  **Constraint Compliance**: The function requires *exactly* `n/m` items per pack. Therefore, we cannot perform 2-for-1 swaps (which change item counts), but we can perform 2-for-2 swaps.
3.  **Efficiency Trade-off**: 2-for-2 swaps have $O(G^4)$ complexity (where $G$ is groups per pack). To afford this, I reduce `num_restarts` from 2048 to 256 and prune the candidates to `num_refined=8` (instead of 64) before running the expensive refinement. This keeps the total FLOPs reasonable.
4.  **Vectorization**: The 2-swap logic is fully vectorized using precomputed upper-triangular indices to generate all pairs, and broadcasting to compute swap gains for all candidate pairs in parallel.
</DESCRIPTION>
